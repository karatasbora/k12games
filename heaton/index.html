The Cloze Procedure

Testing Overall Reading Comprehension

Section Overview: The Cloze procedure involves deleting words from a text at regular intervals (e.g., every 7th word) and requiring the student to replace them. It is a highly reliable measure of overall language proficiency and reading comprehension. This section allows you to interact with a sample Cloze test and visualizes the statistical impact of utilizing the "Exact Word" scoring method versus the "Acceptable Word" scoring method, a key debate highlighted in Chapter 9.

The Correlation Principle (r ≈ 0.95)

Heaton notes a critical psychometric finding: While "Acceptable Word" scoring yields higher absolute scores, it correlates almost perfectly with "Exact Word" scoring. Notice how the rank order of Students A through E never changes, even when you adjust the test difficulty using the demo on the right.

Test Administration & Marking

Ensuring Reliability and Standardized Conditions

Section Overview: Even the most valid test is useless if it is administered poorly or marked inconsistently. This section focuses on the practicalities of testing environments and scorer reliability. You will explore the concept of "Marker Drift"—how a rater's severity can change over a long marking session—and review essential administrative checklists derived from Chapter 10 to ensure a standardized testing environment.

Admin Checklist

  • ✓ Invigilation instructions clear and uniform.
  • ✓ Acoustic conditions verified for listening tests.
  • ✓ Candidate spacing to prevent copying.
  • ✓ Clear instructions on guessing penalty (if any).
  • ✓ Double-marking procedure established for essays.

Statistical Proof of Test Efficacy

Section Overview: How do we know a test question is actually "good"? Chapter 11 introduces the quantitative backbone of language testing: Item Analysis. This section provides an interactive environment to understand Item Facility (how easy an item is) and Item Discrimination (how well an item separates high-ability from low-ability students). You will interact with a scatter plot that maps the optimal "zone" for test items and use a dynamic calculator to compute Facility Values.
